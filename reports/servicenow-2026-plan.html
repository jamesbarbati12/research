Analyst Summary

2026: The year ServiceNow proves that AI is not just a feature — it's a new revenue stream.

What's working: Q1 beat the high end of guidance on every metric — subscription revenue grew 22%, cRPO hit $12.64B (+22.5%), and Now Assist customers paying over $1M in ACV grew 130% year-over-year. The AI monetization thesis is no longer theoretical. It's in the numbers.

The big bet: Two security acquisitions (Armis + Veza) closed in Q1, creating the only unified platform that can discover every enterprise asset, govern every identity, and automate every security response through a single workflow engine. Management guided these will compress margins in 2026 but expects AI efficiencies from "Now on Now" to normalize margin expansion trajectories in FY2027.

What to watch: The Board authorized an additional $5 billion for share repurchases with a $2B accelerated share repurchase executed immediately — signaling management believes the stock is undervalued at current levels. The Q2 report (~July 22) will be the first true test of Armis integration and whether the 130% Now Assist growth rate is sustainable or a one-quarter surge.

The verdict: ServiceNow's 2026 plan is aggressive but credible. A 22% growth company trading at 24x forward earnings, generating 32%+ FCF margins, with $12.6B in contracted near-term backlog, executing beat-and-raise for 7+ consecutive quarters — the only real question is when the market re-rates, not if.
